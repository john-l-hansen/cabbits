# The Bookshelf

The Bookshelf is the primary gateway to learning within Cabbits.

It is not a list of lessons.

It is a living library of adventures, stories, puzzles, and discoveries that grow alongside the player.

Children should feel like they are choosing the next adventure to experience with their Cabbit.

---

# Interaction

Tap the bookshelf.
The camera gently zooms toward it.
Books slide outward onto a wooden shelf.
The Cabbit walks over and sits beside the player, excited to read.
A warm page-turn animation introduces the library.

---

# Library Layout

Rather than a traditional grid, books appear on physical shelves.
Books feel collectible.
Each cover is unique.

---

# Book Categories

- **Stories**: Reading adventures and comprehension.
- **Logic**: Puzzles, Patterns, Sequencing, Critical thinking.
- **Mathematics**: Counting, Addition, Subtraction, Geometry, Money, Measurement.
- **Language**: Vocabulary, Grammar, Spelling, Writing.
- **Science**: Discovery, Nature, Space, Animals, Experiments.
- **Creativity**: Drawing, Music, Storytelling, Building.

---

# Book Structure

Every book is a self-contained adventure.
Each contains:
- Introduction
- Chapters
- Mini-games
- Interactive moments
- Rewards
- Story conclusion

Books should vary greatly in style (stories, mysteries, puzzle books, activity books).

---

# Reading Together

The Cabbit actively participates:
- Looks at illustrations.
- Points at objects.
- Asks simple questions.
- Celebrates correct answers.
- Gets excited by discoveries.
- Falls asleep during bedtime stories.

The experience should feel shared rather than instructional.

---

# Difficulty

Books adapt to the player.
- Younger players receive: More illustrations, larger text, simpler vocabulary, fewer choices.
- Older players receive: Longer chapters, more complex puzzles, richer vocabulary, multi-step challenges.
Difficulty should adjust invisibly so children simply feel successful.

---

# Progress

Each completed chapter places a bookmark inside the book.
Books slowly fill the shelves.
Favorite books become worn.
Recently read books remain stacked on a nearby table.
The library visually tells the player's learning journey.

---

# AI-Generated Stories

Some books may be dynamically generated:
- The Cabbit visits the player's hometown.
- Stories use the player's name.
- Favorite toys appear.
- Previously discovered characters return.
- Seasonal adventures reflect the real world.
This creates an effectively endless library while maintaining a consistent art style and educational framework.

---

# Parent Layer

Behind each book exists an educational profile tracking:
- Reading comprehension
- Vocabulary growth
- Logic skills
- Mathematical concepts
- Areas needing reinforcement
- Interests
- Time spent
This data helps recommend future books without making learning feel like assessment.
